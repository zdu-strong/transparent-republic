# Citizen Assessment

No one is born a citizen. Even the children of citizens are only considered immigrants at birth, and they must pass an assessment in order to become citizens.

## Types of Legal Status

### 1. Citizen
Entitled to a basic living allowance, sovereign dividends, voting rights, and the right to run for office.<br/>
Holds nationality and a passport. Dual or multiple citizenship is not permitted.<br/>

### 2. Immigrant
Entitled to a basic living allowance.<br/>
Holds nationality and a passport. Dual or multiple citizenship is not permitted.<br/>

### 3. Resident

Resident can live there, buy land, and do business.

### 4. Anonymous

Anonymous can buy a receipt printer and start doing business.

## Citizenship Assessment Requirements

1. Must be at least 18 years old.<br/>
2. Must have held immigrant status for at least 18 years.<br/>
3. Must have received the Basic Living Security Grant for at least 18 years.<br/>
4. Must be able to speak the country’s language.<br/>
5. Must understand the political system:<br/>
A 20-question multiple-choice test, with a passing score of 60%.<br/>

## Quantity restriction

Resources are limited, so the number of residents who can become immigrants each day is limited and determined by random selection.<br/>

There is no limit on the number of residents or citizens.<br/>

However, human conscience must not grow cold. A Special Rescue Department shall therefore be established to help people whose suffering receives exceptional media attention.<br/>

## Supplementary Explanation

Citizens retain full citizenship rights even after leaving for another country. They can still vote, receive the basic living security payment, and receive sovereign dividends.<br/>

Immigrants are entitled to the basic living security payment only when they are staying in the Transparent Republic.<br/>
