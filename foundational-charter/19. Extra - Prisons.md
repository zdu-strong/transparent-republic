# Prisons

## Prisons are divided into high-security areas and low-security areas.<br/>

### Low-security areas

Prisoners in low-security areas may choose each day to use prisoner-only facilities such as libraries, basketball courts, football fields, and other activity spaces.<br/>

### High-security areas

Prisoners in high-security areas may be transferred to low-security areas if they commit no violations for 1 month.<br/>

If a prisoner threatens another person, the case shall be heard by a court. The prisoner shall receive an additional one-month sentence and be assigned one dedicated correctional officer for supervision. The prisoner may communicate with other prisoners under the officer’s supervision, and the officer shall intervene to stop the prisoner from using threatening language.<br/>

If a prisoner attempts to harm or harms another person, the case shall be heard by a court. The prisoner shall receive an additional three-month sentence and be assigned three dedicated correctional officers for supervision. The prisoner shall be prohibited from approaching other prisoners.<br/>

## Special criminal provisions for offenses committed by prisoners inside prisons

The law shall establish special criminal provisions for offenses committed by prisoners inside prisons.<br/>

If a prisoner threatens or harms another person, the case shall be tried by a court. The prisoner shall receive an increased sentence and be transferred to high-security areas.<br/>

Inmates in high-security areas have thirty minutes of unrestricted internet access each day. If their relatives are unable to contact them, they may sue the government in court.<br/>

Inmates in low-security areas have unlimited unrestricted internet access.<br/>

## Warden

The country establishes a prison department, and the minister of prisons sends a Forward Triumvirate of three officials to manage each new prison.<br/>


The three officials vote to elect the warden, while the other two officials serve as prison overseers. If the vote totals differ, the official with the most votes becomes the warden. If the vote count is tied, the official ranked higher by the system will be elected as the prison warden.<br/>

The warden is responsible for hiring staff and managing the prison.<br/>

Each year, two new officials are sent to replace the prison overseers. The prison overseers do not take part in management, they are only responsible for oversight. The three officials may hold a vote at any time to elect a new warden.<br/>

The three officials must assess the initial warden. Each assessment shall be positive, negative, or withheld.<br/>
