# Prisons

## Prisons are divided into high-security areas and low-security areas.<br/>

### Low-security areas

Prisoners in low-security areas may choose each day to use prisoner-only facilities such as libraries, basketball courts, football fields, and other activity spaces.<br/>

### High-security areas

Prisoners in high-security areas may be transferred to low-security areas if they commit no violations for 3 months.<br/>

If a prisoner threatens another person, the case shall be tried by a court. The prisoner shall receive an additional 1 month of imprisonment and shall be assigned 1 dedicated prison officer for supervision.<br/>

If a prisoner attempts to harm another person, the case shall be tried by a court. The prisoner shall receive an additional 3 months of imprisonment and shall be assigned 3 dedicated prison officers for supervision.<br/>

If a prisoner harms another person, the case shall be tried by a court. The prisoner shall receive an additional 6 months of imprisonment and shall be assigned 6 dedicated prison officers for supervision.<br/>

The number of dedicated prison officers assigned to supervise a prisoner shall decrease by 1 each month. When the number of assigned prison officers is greater than or equal to 2, the prisoner is prohibited from approaching other prisoners. When the number of assigned prison officers is 1, the prisoner may communicate with other prisoners under the supervision of a prison officer, and the prison officer shall stop the prisoner from using threatening language.<br/>

## Special criminal provisions for offenses committed by prisoners inside prisons

The law shall establish special criminal provisions for offenses committed by prisoners inside prisons.<br/>

If a prisoner threatens or harms another person, the case shall be tried by a court. The prisoner shall receive an increased sentence and be transferred to high-security areas.<br/>


Inmates in the heavy-security section have thirty minutes of free internet access each day. If their relatives are unable to contact them, they may sue the government in court.<br/>

Inmates in the light-security section have unlimited free internet access.<br/>

## Warden

The country establishes a prison department, and the Minister of Prisons sends a three-official team to manage each new prison.<br/>

The three officials vote to elect the warden, while the other two officials serve as prison supervisors. If the vote totals differ, the official with the most votes becomes the warden. If the vote totals are tied, the official who arrived at the prison earliest becomes the warden.<br/>

The warden is responsible for hiring staff and managing the prison.<br/>

Each year, two new officials are sent to replace the prison supervisors. The prison supervisors do not take part in management; they are only responsible for oversight. The three officials may hold a vote at any time to elect a new warden.<br/>
