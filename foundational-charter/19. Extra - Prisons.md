# Prisons

## Prisons are divided into high-security areas and low-security areas.<br/>

### Low-security areas

Prisoners in low-security areas may choose each day to use prisoner-only facilities such as libraries, basketball courts, football fields, and other activity spaces.<br/>

### High-security areas

Prisoners in high-security areas may be transferred to a low-security prison if they commit no violations for 3 months.<br/>

If a prisoner threatens another person, the case shall be tried by a court. The prisoner shall receive an additional 1 month of imprisonment and shall be assigned 1 dedicated police officer for supervision.<br/>

If a prisoner attempts to harm another person, the case shall be tried by a court. The prisoner shall receive an additional 3 months of imprisonment and shall be assigned 3 dedicated police officers for supervision.<br/>

If a prisoner harms another person, the case shall be tried by a court. The prisoner shall receive an additional 6 months of imprisonment and shall be assigned 6 dedicated police officers for supervision.<br/>

The number of dedicated police officers assigned to supervise a prisoner shall decrease by 1 each month. When the number of assigned police officers is greater than or equal to 2, the prisoner is prohibited from approaching other prisoners. When the number of assigned police officers is 1, the prisoner may communicate with other prisoners under police supervision, and the police officer shall stop the prisoner from using threatening language.<br/>

## Special legal provisions for prisoners inside prisons

The law shall establish special legal provisions for prisoners inside prisons.<br/>

If a prisoner threatens or harms another person, the case shall be tried by a court. The prisoner shall receive an increased sentence and be transferred to a high-security prison.<br/>
