# All Governance Regions

**Governance region** can act like a broad umbrella, covering states, counties, cities, towns, townships, villages, boroughs, and local communities without getting trapped in one country’s exact administrative vocabulary.<br/>

Every governance region, from states and counties down to towns, townships, villages, boroughs, and local communities, should have sufficient autonomy.<br/>

The government establishes an **Autonomy Department** to identify governance regions that have not held democratic elections or achieved self-governance. The department recruits officials and sends them, in groups of three, to live in these governance regions.<br/>

These officials will organize elections and manage these regions. Local citizens may participate in democratic elections at any time and exercise self-governance.<br/>

Citizens may hold multiple identities at the same time, such as **Community Supervisor**, **National Supervisory Council member**, government official, and others.<br/>
