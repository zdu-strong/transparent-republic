# Freedom for Officials

Corruption and abuse of power, when used as broad political or moral accusations, are not crimes under the law.<br/>

Officials must have freedom to govern, make decisions, use lawful authority, and make political mistakes. A former official must not be destroyed by a later government merely because the later government describes past decisions as corruption or abuse of power.<br/>

Embezzlement is a criminal offense under the law. Prosecution must be initiated within three months of the embezzlement, or within three months after the relevant public financial records become available. After that period, the court will no longer accept or hear the case.<br/>

Political dissatisfaction should be solved by dismissal, replacement, public review, and institutional reform, not by turning vague accusations into criminal weapons against former officials.<br/>
