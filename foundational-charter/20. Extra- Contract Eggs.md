
# Contract Eggs

The government signs contracts with egg companies that specify the subsidy amount, the minimum quantity of eggs to be supplied, the maximum egg price, quality standards, and penalties for violations.<br/>

The government may sign contracts with multiple egg companies, and the subsidy amount may differ from one company to another.<br/>

If an egg company violates the contract, the government may refuse to renew it.<br/>

Egg companies that do not sign a contract are still free to sell eggs.<br/>

Egg companies may be allowed to monopolize the market, with egg products exempted from antitrust laws.<br/>
