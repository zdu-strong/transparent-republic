#  Healthcare Coverage

Healthcare coverage is divided into three categories: minor illnesses, moderate illnesses, and serious illnesses.<br/>

For minor illnesses, the state pays 70% of the medical cost, while the individual pays 30%. Anyone may receive this treatment.<br/>

For moderate illnesses, the state pays the full cost. Only citizens and immigrants are eligible for this treatment. Others must pay out of pocket or purchase commercial healthcare coverage.<br/>

For serious illnesses, the state sets a certain coverage limit. Medical costs within that limit are paid by the state. If treatment continues beyond the coverage limit, the individual must pay the excess cost. Only citizens and immigrants are eligible for this treatment. Others must pay out of pocket or purchase commercial healthcare coverage.<br/>
