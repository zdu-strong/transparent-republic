# Tax System

The central government collects taxes centrally and allocates 30% of the tax revenue from each Governance Region to the Governance Regions.<br/>
If a Governance Region deems 30% insufficient, it must submit a detailed budget for review by its finance team, after which additional funds will be allocated.<br/>
Governance Regions have full control over their finances, and the central government cannot interfere.<br/>
Taxes include consumption tax, commodity profit tax, property tax.<br/>
All goods are subject to a unified commodity profit tax rate and a unified consumption tax rate, while different cost ratios are assigned to different product categories. For small businesses, product categories are not distinguished; instead, a fixed cost ratio is assigned based on the type of business.<br/>
Abolishing personal income tax will allow capital to flow freely.<br/>

No tariffs anymore.<br/>
Products with security risks should be banned from entering the country.<br/>
For low-price dumping products, subsidies should be used to ensure the survival of the domestic industrial chain.<br/>

Property tax:<br/>
All fixed assets (such as Land, luxury vehicles, aircraft, and yachts) will be subject to a fixed asset tax on a monthly basis according to a specified ratio.<br/>
Fixed assets are only subject to fixed asset tax. No consumption tax or goods profit tax is generated during transactions.<br/>
The taxable base value of a fixed asset is calculated according to the value corresponding to its fixed asset type, regardless of the actual transaction price.<br/>

Land:<br/>
The government classifies land and determines land types. The government, individuals, and companies may only use land according to the permitted use of its land type.<br/>
Land types are divided into public land and commercial land.<br/>
Public land may only be used by the government and is not subject to fixed asset tax.<br/>
Commercial land owned by the government is also not subject to fixed asset tax, but the government may not use commercial land and may only sell it through public auction.<br/>
The government has the authority to adjust land types. When the owner of the land is not the government, the land type may only be adjusted to a higher-value land type. Its permitted use may not be changed, and each adjustment must include a purchase contract, allowing the owner to decide whether to sell.<br/>
The government may sell land, revenue from land sales belongs to the national fiscal revenue.<br/>
Buildings on the land may not be sold separately. They may only be rented out. Rent is not subject to tax.<br/>
When a landowner is unable to pay the fixed asset tax, the land will be auctioned. The tenants’ lease contracts will become invalid. The proceeds from the auction will first be used to compensate long-term tenants for their rent, and the remaining amount will be returned to the landowner.<br/>

Virtual goods:<br/>
Virtual goods are tax-exempt, but physical gifts must first be registered with the government to determine the value of the goods and the applicable tax amount.<br/>
