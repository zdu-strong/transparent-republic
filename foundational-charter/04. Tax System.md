The central government collects taxes centrally and allocates 30% of the tax revenue from each state to the states.<br/>
If a state deems 30% insufficient, it must submit a detailed budget for review by its finance team, after which additional funds will be allocated.<br/>
States have full control over their finances, and the central government cannot interfere.<br/>
Taxes include consumption tax, commodity profit tax, property tax.<br/>
All goods are subject to a unified commodity profit tax rate and a unified consumption tax rate, while different cost ratios are assigned to different product categories. For small businesses, product categories are not distinguished; instead, a fixed cost ratio is assigned based on the type of business.<br/>
Abolishing personal income tax will allow capital to flow freely.<br/>

No tariffs anymore.<br/>
Products with security risks should be banned from entering the country.<br/>
For low-price dumping products, subsidies should be used to ensure the survival of the domestic industrial chain.<br/>

Property tax:<br/>
All fixed assets (such as houses, cars, and yachts) will be subject to a fixed asset tax on a monthly basis according to a specified ratio.<br/>


Land:<br/>
The government may sell land, and land is subject to fixed asset tax.<br/>
Buildings on the land may not be sold separately. They may only be rented out.<br/>
When a landowner is unable to pay the fixed asset tax, the land will be auctioned. The tenants’ lease contracts will become invalid. The proceeds from the auction will first be used to compensate long-term tenants for their rent, and the remaining amount will be returned to the landowner.<br/>
Government land use must also begin with the purchase of land, and the government must also pay fixed asset tax.<br/>
When the government purchases unoccupied land, the price is calculated based on the unit price of that type of land and the land area.<br/>
Revenue from land sales belongs to the national fiscal revenue.<br/>
